# MeetFlow Privacy Notice

Last updated: July 3, 2026

MeetFlow is designed as a local-first desktop meeting assistant. Meeting audio, transcripts, notes, summaries, preferences, and imported files are handled by the desktop application on your machine unless you explicitly configure an external provider.

## Local-First Data Handling

- Audio recordings are created and stored through the desktop runtime.
- Transcript and meeting data are stored locally by the app.
- Imported audio is processed by local conversion and transcription workflows.
- Summary generation can use local models or a provider you configure.
- MeetFlow does not require cloud upload for its core recording and transcription workflow.

## Optional Analytics

Usage analytics is optional and off by default. If enabled, MeetFlow may collect product and performance telemetry such as:

- App version and platform
- Feature usage events
- Error categories and performance timings
- Anonymous session identifiers

MeetFlow analytics does not collect:

- Meeting audio
- Transcript text
- Meeting notes or summaries
- Meeting titles or participant names
- Imported file contents

Analytics can be disabled from the app settings.

## External Providers

If you configure an external AI provider, requests to that provider are governed by the provider's own privacy and data handling terms. Local model workflows remain on-device.

## Data Control

You control the data stored by MeetFlow on your device. You can manage recordings, meeting entries, preferences, model settings, and imported audio through the app and platform file locations.

## Security Notes

- Local files inherit the protections of your operating system account and storage configuration.
- Keep your device, app runtime, and model/provider credentials protected.
- Remove recordings and meeting data you no longer need according to your own retention requirements.

## Contact

For privacy questions, contact the project maintainer through the distribution channel where you received MeetFlow.
